Fulbright Program

English Teaching Assistant

September 2016—June 2017
Madrid, Spain

As a Fulbright English teaching assistant, I was charged with not only week-to-week lesson planning, but also with fostering a sense tolerance, mutual understanding, and a desire to learn. My work extended beyond the classroom, as I worked in food kitchens throughout the city to serve and to gain a greater understanding of the role of food in Spanish culture.

  • Assisting with classes in San Martín de la Vega.
  • Preparing a group of students for a city-wide competition by focusing on critical thinking, debate, and creative problem-solving.

Graphiq

Content Coordinator

January 2016—June 2016
Santa Barbara, CA

As Content Coordinator at Graphiq, I maintained a variety of responsibilities, from editing content for the company's most valuable partnership to managing a team of freelance writers.

  • Edited freelance content as well as internal media (totaling more than 150 pieces)
  • Created, refined, and managed the editing and feedback loop with our team of freelance writers.
  • Owned a transition to new task management software to be used by the Editorial team, other teams within Graphiq, and external stakeholders (more than 75 users).
  • Contributed to the construction of each month’s Editorial calendar.

White House

White House Fellows Intern

May 2015—August 2015
Washington, D.C.

While working as an intern in the Office of the White House Fellows, I provided administrative support to a team of four and completed the most thorough analysis of the program during the Obama administration up to that point.

  • Edited freelance content as well as internal media (totaling more than 150 pieces)
  • Collected, analyzed, and presented data in a memo for senior staff members and the President’s Commission on White House Fellowships.
  • Composed memos for senior staff members that explored methods for improving outreach methods for the Fellowship.
  • Composed standard operating procedures for future interns and employees.
  • Facilitated and helped coordinate the logistics of the biggest annual event for the White House Fellows Office.

Hattiesburg Metropolitan
Planning Organization

GIS & Graphics Intern

January 2015—May 2015
August 2015—November 2015
Hattiesburg, MS

During my time as a GIS intern, I focused primarily on the production of paper maps fit for public use highlighted neighborhoods in the city as well as local attractions. We used them not only to introduce citizens, new and old, to features of the city, but also to generate interest on social media for other local events.

  • Created logos and banners (print and digital media) for the Metropolitan Planning Organization and our partners, including the University of Southern Mississippi and Mississippi Department of Transportation.
  • Created visually appealing maps of various neighborhoods within the city using Adobe Illustrator and ArcGIS.
  • Collected and created GIS data.
  • Helped facilitate the move to an updated pedestrian pathways and public transit system.

Historic Hattiesburg
Downtown Association

Community Development Intern

May 2014—August 2014
Hattiesburg, MS

While serving as a Community Development intern, I focused on engaging students with the downtown district and increasing connectivity between the University of the Southern Mississippi and Historic Hattiesburg Downtown Association (HHDA). In August of 2014, I facilitated the first Honors College Welcome event to be hosted by HHDA as well as its members — the tradition continues today!

  • Handled brand management, connectivity, and economic development to strengthen the link between the University of Southern Mississippi and downtown Hattiesburg.
  • Created visually appealing maps that effectively highlighted relevant buildings, spaces, and parks for students.
  • Launched an initiative between the Honors College and HHDA to encourage greater participation of the students.

University of Southern Mississippi

Graphics intern

Fall 2015

In my final semester on the Study Abroad Peer Advisors team, I was selected to serve as the lead of the Graphics team, during which I focused on creating print and digital content to engage students in various study abroad opportunities offered by the university.

  • Created graphics, flyers, stamps, and posters to further the mission of the Office of International Programs in recruiting students to participate in summer, semester, and year-long study abroad programs.

Spanish Language Tutor

January 2013—December 2014
(Academic Terms)

After selection by departmental faculty, I worked in the Language Lab to help students of all levels with their Spanish. During my time with the team, I was awarded the award for the Most Outstanding Spanish student of the year.

  • Helped students strengthen abilities with conversation and grammar in the Spanish language with a group of tutors selected by the Department of Foreign Languages and Literatures.
